My career has taken me from shipboard engineering and defense contracting into technical support and Information Technology. Each step has helped me build a different part of the foundation I am bringing into my IT and cybersecurity career.

My Professional Path

I did not enter Information Technology through one traditional route. I first built experience maintaining complex equipment, following strict procedures, documenting work, and troubleshooting problems under pressure. I later made a deliberate career change into IT while returning to school and gaining hands-on support experience through Geek Squad.

Gas Turbine Systems Technician

United States Navy
2017–2021

I joined the Navy as an undesignated engineering sailor before choosing the rate (job) of Gas Turbine Systems Technician Machanical (GSM) and advancing to E-4. I worked around complex mechanical and technical systems worth millions of dollars aboard two ships in demanding engineering environments.

The work required system diagnostics, preventative maintenance, accurate logs, technical documentation, and strict operational and safety procedures. Engineering personnel also carried major emergency response responsibilities, so staying calm, communicating clearly, and completing work correctly were always important.

I took initiative in daily work and preventative maintenance while helping coordinate tasks and report progress to leadership. I also completed annual cybersecurity training and followed security practices for system access and data protection.

Outside Machinist

Aerotek supporting Huntington Ingalls Industries
2021–2023

After leaving active duty military life, I continued working in a technical industrial environment as an outside machinist. I maintained and repaired complex mechanical systems while supporting large shipbuilding projects in Newport News, Virginia.

The job required coordination with engineering teams and other trades. We had to diagnose problems, complete work within project timelines, document what was done, and follow strict technical and safety procedures.

I also worked in a secure environment that required a U.S. Secret Security Clearance. This experience strengthened my ability to work responsibly around sensitive operations and high-value equipment.

Consultation Agent

Best Buy – Geek Squad
2023–Present

Moving into Geek Squad was a deliberate step toward the career I wanted to build. The position gave me direct experience diagnosing hardware, software, and network issues across customer devices while I continued my education in Information Technology.

I provide system setup, Windows-based troubleshooting, and general technical support in a fast-paced environment. The role has also helped me become better at explaining technical problems in a way that makes sense to people with different levels of experience.

I regularly balance multiple clients and tasks while helping support team workflow. This experience has strengthened both my troubleshooting process and my ability to communicate professionally.

Education and Career Transition

I earned my Associate of Applied Business in Information Technology from the University of Cincinnati Blue Ash College. The program gave me a strong foundation in networking, operating systems, programming, databases, and technical support while preparing me to continue into the bachelor's program.

I am currently pursuing my Bachelor of Science in Information Technology with a concentration in Cybersecurity at the University of Cincinnati. As I continue my education, I am expanding my knowledge of networking, system administration, cybersecurity, cloud technologies, and other areas of modern IT. My expected graduation is August 2028.

Combining my education with nearly three years of experience at Geek Squad has allowed me to apply what I learn in the classroom to real-world technical support. Along with my engineering background in the Navy and defense contracting, these experiences have strengthened my troubleshooting, communication, documentation, and problem-solving skills while preparing me for a career in Information Technology and Cybersecurity.
